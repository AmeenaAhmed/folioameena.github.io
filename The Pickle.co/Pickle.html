The Pickle Co.

Website re-design of a popular pickle subscription company.

Objectives

The Pickle Co, a popular and in demand pickle subscription called for a rebrand of the site to increase traffic to the website and to impove the user experince , below are outlined the goals of the makers.


○ Improve the E-commerce experience.
○ Enhance Company Branding.
○ Increase Sales.
○ Increase Customer satisfaction.



The UX Research





Target Audience


Almost everyone likes pickles (86%), especially Baby Boomers (70%),Forty-one percent of Americans say their favorite way to eat pickles is on a sandwich or burger, though straight from the jar is a close second (39%). Interestingly, busy households with kids ages 13-17 are more likely to eat them right out of the jar (42%) vs. on a sandwich (34%).(source cision news) This information helps in the identification of the target demographic and the creation of an interface that appeals to young people while being simple enough for the elder generation to use.According to this statistic, 245.85 million Americans consumed pickles in 2020. This figure is projected to increase to 252.73 million in 2025.Pickle sales are still strong and don't appear to be slowing down anytime soon.

The Pickle Co. Branding


The original website did not have a set company branding, information was placed in a somewhat systematic way however it was not user focused and did not create the visual experience that would best suit the consumer. The client wanted to keep the same packaging for the products but have it correlate with the website, for this reason, purple was chosen as the initial and main color used in the website as it best correlated with the original packaging but also made the main item (pickles) standout due to green and purple being opposite on the color wheel.

A minimalistic approach was taken which was very product-centered, as too much clutter and an overabundance of information can be confusing for the customer.
