Helping Hand

Mobile Application designed to help COVID patients have access to urgent medical and food requirements while allowing communication with friends and family.

What do we do?

Helping hand is an app designed in the midst of the Covid-19 pandemic. It was created for those affected by the virus to have easy access to urgent and critical supplies without making
any physical contact in the comfort of their own homes.

Timeline:March-May (2022)
My Role: Personal Project


Goals,Problem and the Solution


Goals


The project's goals are to establish an environment where users can readily access their needs without having to interact with anyone physically, have a set number of meals, medicine, and access to friends and family to help with the recovery process.

Problem


Allow covid patients to be able to easily access there needs.


Solution


Accessibility is key.

User Interviews


Five user interviews were conducted at the begging of this project.


The purpose of the interview was to gain a deeper understanding of the potential users' wants and needs. The information gathered during the interview helped in the development of a solution.


Empathy Mapping


I collected the data from the five users and began empathy mapping, using the user's comments from the interview, I could then articulate the user's emotions to better empathize with the user.

Solution Mapping


At this part of the development process, it was time to face the problems the users were facing with the current design of the application.

Competitive analysis


There aren't many apps that allow a friend or family member to order meals for you; Grabbit and Glovo, for example, are apps that will transport anything to your door, but Henchman can deliver anything you want to anyone you want within 60 minutes.

Low Fidelity Wireframes.

With all the information collected I developed low fidelity wireframes for the new and improved helping hand application. I knew the problems, I knew how the users felt when using the app, I knew the thoughts towards the app and with that, I knew I could design a new application that would benefit the user.







The Application:





Login and Sign up Screen


Main Menu


When you enter the app, there are three screens that lead to various areas of the app offers. There is a tab for food and medicine and to make donations to help those who need it.

Ordering Food.


One in three people infected with COVID-19 lose their appetites enough to skip meals. It's most common in people over 65 where roughly four in ten people (43%) experience a loss in appetite at some point during their illness. Patients have access to fresh meals when meals are planned, making eating more convenient. Allergies to specific foods are also taken into account based on the data. The meals available offer a choice of different items that can be eaten slowly over time or all at once. Friends and family members can also place food orders for the patient. All of the meals are inspected before being served.

Ordering Medicine.


Many covid patients in quarantine take a variety of medication to help them cope with the discomfort of their illness. A medication tab allows the user/friend/family to order both meals and medication at the same time. Furthermore, the pharmaceutical tab provides patients with a no-contact delivery option, which is more convenient for them.

Donation.


More than six in 10 (63%) charities reported that they had taken at least one action towards their reserves in response to the pandemic, with two in five (40%) reporting that they had used their reserves to help them through the crisis.Around a quarter (26%) reviewed their charities' policy towards reserves, and more than one in five (23%) said that they were building their reserves up in case of future crises. By including a donation button, it assists organisations that are assisting people in need during a crisis.

Friends and Family.


To be able to place food and pharmaceutical orders for your friends and family. It is convenient for individuals who are sick and require assistance from afar.
